Childhood Years


I always find it fun to reminisce about my childhood. Back to the days where the only things I had in mind were silly and childish ideas.


We really did not have a lot in retrospect. I grew up in a simple family looking back.

My father, working hard and making ends meet. My mother, a woman of many things, manages a myriad of things. I, on the other hand, is nothing but an uncontrollable child with infinite energy.


How I miss those days... Playing around all day and night. No responsibilities to think of.

I also did pretty well in grade school. I was consistent with all of my grades. Disciplined enough to make it to the cream of the crop.


I remember my parents being really proud of my achievements when I was a kid. Their smiles radiating pure joy.

Teenage Years


All of the crazy things happened here if my memory serves me right. I accomplised quite a lot, academically speaking


I joined quite a handful of contests, both school-wide and division, even regionals! I once joined a web design context for 2017 Division Technolympics in Marikina and placed second bringing home a silver medal.

I also did campus journaling. Writing really is my passion. I like to creatively account what I am feeling.


In retrospect, I was consistent in attending trainings and seminars to really improve my writing.

In Tenth Grade, I was selected to compete for the annual research festival. I had two other groupmates, which was hard because of the difference in our personalities.


Nonetheless, I am happy to share that we won. It was a great experience!

College Years


It has been a roller coaster ride for me, recently. College really is a challenge. More so in an F2F set-up because I had to travel from Marikina to Manila.


However taxing it may be, it was still fun because of the friends I made in school.

I joined the UE Dawn in my freshman year. I was not really expecting much since I know that am up againts better writers.


But the I was given a chance by the committee to be a part of UE's accreditted student publication.

I am certain that there are still a lot of things ahead of me for I still have two years left of tertiary education. A lot can still happen.


And I am hoping with all my heart that it will be worthwhile and fun.
